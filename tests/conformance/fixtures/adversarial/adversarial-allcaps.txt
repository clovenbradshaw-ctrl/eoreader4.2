ANNUAL COMPLIANCE REVIEW

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Compliance Office completed its annual review of purchasing practices across all departments.¹ The review found that the Public Works Department, led by Carla Fenwick, substantially complied with procurement policy.² Two exceptions were noted in the Parks and Recreation Department.

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

Finding 1 involved a purchase order issued without competitive bid, in apparent conflict with Section 4.2 of the Purchasing Manual.³ Finding 2 involved a delayed disclosure by a Council member, discussed further below.⁴

METHODOLOGY

Auditors reviewed a sample of 240 purchase orders issued between July 2024 and December 2024, interviewed department heads including Fenwick, Sadiq, and Okonkwo, and cross-referenced vendor payment records maintained by Corvane Systems.

CONCLUSION

The Compliance Office recommends that the Council adopt the attached policy revisions no later than its next regular meeting.

¹ See Compliance Office Charter, adopted 2019.
² Fenwick provided a written response, attached as Appendix B.
³ The Purchasing Manual was last revised in 2022.
⁴ See discussion of Resolution 2025-14, supra.
