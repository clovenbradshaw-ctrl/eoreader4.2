COUNCIL MEMBER'S ABSTENTION ON PAVING CONTRACT DRAWS SCRUTINY

By Teodora Marsh

FAIRVIEW HEIGHTS — A city council member's decision to abstain from a vote authorizing additional payments to a paving contractor has drawn renewed attention to the city's conflict-of-interest disclosure rules.

Council Member Harold Kim abstained from the March 11 vote on a change order for the Elm Street resurfacing project, citing his employer's business relationship with a subcontractor on the job. Kim has not publicly named the subcontractor or described the nature of his employer's relationship with it.

City Attorney Renata Blackwood said Kim's abstention satisfied the disclosure requirements of the city's ethics ordinance, which requires a council member to abstain — but does not require public disclosure of the underlying relationship — when a conflict exists.

Director of Public Works Carla Fenwick, who presented the change order to the Council, said the additional $84,200 was needed to address drainage issues discovered during construction. Fenwick said the contractor, Ridgeline Paving, had been cooperative in identifying the problem.

Resident Priya Nair, who has closely followed the Elm Street project because of ongoing noise complaints near her home, said she wanted more transparency from the Council about conflicts of interest generally, not just Kim's.

Kim declined to comment beyond his statement at the March 11 meeting. Mayor Patricia Owusu said she had no concerns about how the abstention was handled.

The Elm Street project is expected to be substantially complete by the end of April.
