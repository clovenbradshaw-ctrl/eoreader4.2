FAIRVIEW HEIGHTS ADVANCES WATER SYSTEM OVERHAUL AFTER YEARS OF DELAY

By Teodora Marsh

FAIRVIEW HEIGHTS — The City Council voted Tuesday night to move forward with a long-delayed overhaul of the city's water treatment control system, a project officials say is needed to prevent a repeat of the outages that struck the city's east side last summer.

The vote came after Utilities Director Marguerite Okonkwo told the Council that the existing control system, installed more than two decades ago, "is being held together by parts nobody makes anymore." Okonkwo said the manufacturer, Corvane Systems, stopped supporting the equipment in 2019.

Council Member Daniel Reyes, who has pushed for the upgrade since he was elected in 2022, called the vote "long overdue." Reyes said residents in his district had raised concerns about water pressure drops for years before last summer's outage finally forced the issue.

Not every council member was convinced the timeline announced by staff was realistic. Council Member Harold Kim questioned whether the Purchasing Division, led by Omar Sadiq, could complete the competitive bidding process before the start of the next fiscal year. Sadiq told the Council his office intended to issue the request for proposals within thirty days.

The project is expected to cost between $2.1 million and $2.6 million, according to estimates presented by Okonkwo. Mayor Patricia Owusu said the city planned to fund the work using a combination of reserve funds and a state infrastructure grant.

Plant Manager Desmond Prewitt, who has worked at the Fairview Heights Water Treatment Facility for eighteen years, said in an interview after the meeting that he was relieved the Council had finally acted. "We've been patching this system for longer than some of my staff have been alive," Prewitt said.

The Council's vote authorizes staff to begin the procurement process. A contract award is not expected before late summer.
